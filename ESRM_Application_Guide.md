# A Simple Guide to the ESRM Application

Welcome! If you're wondering what this whole "ESRM" app is about, you're in the right place. We're going to break down the entire app, page by page, in plain English.

**What is ESRM?**
ESRM stands for **Environmental and Social Risk Management**. Imagine a bank is about to lend a million dollars to a company to build a new factory. Before giving the money, the bank needs to make sure this factory won't pollute a local river (Environmental) or treat its workers poorly (Social). This app is the ultimate checklist and grading sheet to help the bank make safe, responsible decisions.

Let's walk through the main journey of the app, step by step!

---

## The Main Journey: The 6-Step Workflow

When a new project (like building that factory) comes in, the app takes you through a 6-step journey:

### 1. ESS Screening (The "Red Flag" Check)

**What it is:** This is the front door. Before we do any hard math, we just ask some basic questions. Is this project on our "Exclusion List"? (For example, we might have a strict rule: "We never fund illegal logging or weapons manufacturing").
**What happens here:** If the project involves something totally forbidden, the app raises a red flag and stops the project immediately. If it's safe, we move to the next room.

### 2. Risk Categorization (Grading the Risk)

**What it is:** This is where the app acts like a teacher grading a test. We look at different dimensions—like what industry the project is in, the client's past track record, and specific safety standards.
**What happens here:** The app does some cool math behind the scenes and gives the project a grade:

- **Category A (High Risk):** Needs a lot of attention.
- **Category B (Medium Risk):** Needs some attention.
- **Category C (Low Risk):** Easy breezy.

### 3. ESDD (Environmental and Social Due Diligence)

**What it is:** "Due Diligence" is just a fancy way of saying "doing our homework." Since we know the risk category now, we need to gather all the required documents and evidence.
**What happens here:** You'll fill out deeper questionnaires, upload files (like environmental impact reports), and truly investigate the project to make sure everything is legal and safe.

### 4. ESAP (Environmental and Social Action Plan)

**What it is:** Nobody is perfect. Sometimes the homework in the ESDD step shows that the company has a few things to fix. ESAP is the "Fix-It" plan.
**What happens here:** We create a to-do list for the client. "You need to buy better water filters for your factory by October," or "You must establish a worker complaint hotline." We agree on this plan before they get the money.

### 5. Appraisal

**What it is:** The final boss level. We've gathered all the data, scored the risk, done the homework, and made a fix-it plan. Now the big bosses need to look over everything.
**What happens here:** Reviewers and Approvers look at a neat summary of steps 1 through 4. If everything looks good, they hit "Approve," and the project gets the green light to proceed!

### 6. Monitoring

**What it is:** Just because they got the money doesn't mean we stop caring. We have to keep an eye on them over the years to make sure they are actually following the ESAP to-do list.
**What happens here:** This page is used to check in on the project every few months or years, tracking their progress and making sure no new problems pop up.

## The Main ESRM Pages

Here are the main pages you will use in the ESRM app every day:

### 📊 Dashboard (The Big Picture)

**What it is:** When you first log in, you land here. It's like the dashboard of a car.
**What it's for:** It gives you a quick snapshot of everything happening at once. You can see how many projects are High Risk, how many are waiting for your approval, and colorful charts that show the overall health of all the projects the bank is funding.

### ➕ New Project (Where It All Begins)

**What it is:** The starting line.
**What it's for:** When a new business comes asking for a loan (like our factory example), you click here to enter their basic details and start them on the 6-step journey (from ESS Screening to Appraisal).

### 📝 Pending Tasks (Your To-Do List)

**What it is:** The inbox on your desk.
**What it's for:** Instead of searching around trying to figure out what needs to be done next, this page simply lists the things waiting for _you_. If a teammate finishes step 2 and needs you to do step 3, it pops up right here so you know exactly what to work on.

### ✅ Completed Projects (The History Book)

**What it is:** The filing cabinet.
**What it's for:** Once a project makes it all the way through the 6-step journey and gets final approval, it moves here. It’s a safe place to look back at old deals, see what grades they got, and read the documents that were uploaded months or years ago.

### 📘 Methodology (The Rulebook)

**What it is:** The teacher's grading key.
**What it's for:** If someone asks, "Why did this project get a 'High Risk' grade?", this page has the answer. It explains all the math, the rules, and the exact formulas the app uses behind the scenes to determine if a project is safe or dangerous.

### 📑 Reports (The Printouts)

**What it is:** The end-of-term report card generator.
**What it's for:** Sometimes the government, top executives, or auditors want to see what the bank has been doing. This page takes all the risk data from the app and puts it into neat, official spreadsheets and documents that you can easily download and share.

---

## Summary

In short, this application is a **digital safety guard**. You start a **New Project**, follow your **Pending Tasks** through the 6-step journey, use the **Methodology** to understand the risk grades, check the **Dashboard** for the big picture, print **Reports** for the bosses, and eventually store everything safely in **Completed Projects**!
